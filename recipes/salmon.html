Salmon with Beurre Blanc

Ingredients

  • 1 salmon seasoned as you please
  • 3 shallots
  • ~ 1/4 (250g) sticks softened salted butter
  • 250 ml dry white wine (I used a white cooking wine instead)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375 F
  2. Get a pot and fill it about a quarter with water
  3. Set this on the stove to start boiling
  4. the shallots as small as you can without mincing
  5. Take a pan and add the white wine and shallots on medium heat
  6. This will sit and reduce. You will want the wine to go down to about 2 tablespoons worth. This will be maybe 10 minutesWhile that's going on take your salmon and season. I used a tray, added about 2 tablespoons of olive oil, set the salmon on that and then added garlic salt and lemon juice for flavor. I also wasn't paying attention and added vanilla extract instead of lemon but only a slight bit. The color difference clicked in my head eventually and didn't ruin my taste. Also not a revolutionary new thing either. Don't do that. Pay attention.
  7. Cover the salmon and add to the oven for 15 minutes
  8. Take the butter and slice it into mini patties like youâ€™d get at a family-style restaurant
  9. When the shallot wine mix has reduced enough, add a glass or non-wimpy bowl to top of the boiling water to create a double boiler. Add the shallot and wine mix to this.
  10. the butter patties one at a time and whisk until it melts before adding the next
  11. You can add a couple at a time, just keep adding until it's all mixed in
  12. Keep this warm while the salmon cooks
  13. After the 15 minutes uncover and cook for another 15. Internal temp should be 145 F
  14. Pour the sauce on your finished salmon
